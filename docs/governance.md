# Governance

*This section supports Principle 10: You use these principles alongside your organisation's policies and have the right assurance in place.*

## Overview

Effective governance ensures AI systems are developed, deployed, and operated responsibly, safely, and in alignment with organizational objectives and public service principles.

## AI Governance Board or AI Representation on Existing Board

### Purpose and Function

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Set strategic direction for AI adoption across organization
- Review and approve high-risk or high-impact AI initiatives
- Ensure compliance with ethical principles and legal requirements
- Oversee resource allocation for AI projects
- Monitor organizational AI maturity and capability development

**Composition:**
- Senior leaders from key business areas
- Technical experts (data science, engineering, security)
- Legal and compliance representatives
- Ethics and data protection specialists
- External advisors where appropriate

### Decision-Making Authority

**Board Should Have Authority To:**
- Approve or reject AI project proposals
- Set organizational AI policies and standards
- Allocate budget and resources for AI initiatives
- Escalate significant issues to executive leadership
- Mandate risk mitigation measures

### Meeting Frequency and Structure

**Regular Meetings:**
- Quarterly strategic reviews
- Monthly operational oversight (as needed)
- Ad-hoc meetings for urgent decisions
- Annual comprehensive AI strategy review

## Ethics Committee

### Role and Responsibilities

**Core Functions:**
- Review AI projects for ethical implications
- Develop and maintain ethical guidelines specific to organization
- Provide guidance on complex ethical dilemmas
- Monitor compliance with ethical principles
- Engage with external stakeholders on ethical concerns

**Scope of Review:**
- High-risk AI applications
- Projects involving vulnerable populations
- AI systems with significant societal impact
- Novel AI applications without established precedent
- Cross-departmental AI initiatives

### Committee Composition

**Essential Expertise:**
- Ethics specialists or philosophers
- Legal experts familiar with AI law
- Representatives from affected communities
- Technical AI experts
- Policy professionals
- Academic or external ethics advisors

### Integration with Governance

**Relationship to AI Governance Board:**
- Ethics committee provides recommendations to governance board
- Escalation path for ethical concerns
- Regular reporting on ethical compliance
- Joint review of high-risk projects

## Creating an AI Systems Inventory

### Purpose and Benefits

**Why Maintain an Inventory:**
- Understand full scope of AI use across organization
- Ensure proper oversight and governance
- Support compliance with transparency requirements
- Enable risk management and mitigation
- Facilitate knowledge sharing and best practice adoption

### Inventory Contents

**For Each AI System, Document:**
- System name and description
- Business purpose and objectives
- Technical specifications and architecture
- Data sources and types processed
- Risk assessment and mitigation measures
- Stakeholders and responsible parties
- Deployment status and timeline
- Performance metrics and monitoring

### Inventory Management

**Maintenance Requirements:**
- Regular updates as systems evolve
- New system registration process
- Decommissioning procedures
- Version control and change tracking
- Access controls for sensitive information

**Integration with Other Processes:**
- Link to ATRS reporting where required
- Connect with procurement systems
- Integration with risk registers
- Alignment with data protection records

## Governance Structures for Teams

### Project-Level Governance

**Team Structure Requirements:**
- Clear roles and responsibilities
- Senior Responsible Owner (SRO) designation
- Technical lead and business lead identification
- User research and design representation
- Legal, compliance, and security input

**Decision-Making Processes:**
- Stage-gate reviews at key project milestones
- Go/no-go decision points with clear criteria
- Escalation procedures for significant issues
- Change control processes for scope modifications

### Cross-Team Coordination

**Communities of Practice:**
- AI practitioners network for knowledge sharing
- Regular meetups and learning sessions
- Best practice documentation and sharing
- Cross-pollination of ideas and solutions

**Centers of Excellence:**
- Specialist teams providing guidance and support
- Technical expertise for complex implementations
- Standards development and maintenance
- Training and capability development

### Reporting and Accountability

**Regular Reporting Requirements:**
- Progress reports to governance board
- Risk and issue escalation procedures
- Performance metrics and KPI reporting
- Compliance status updates

**Accountability Framework:**
- Clear ownership of AI-related decisions
- Performance measurement and review
- Learning from failures and successes
- Recognition and reward for good practice

## Managing Risk

### Risk Identification

**Categories of AI Risk:**
- Technical risks (bias, accuracy, security)
- Legal and compliance risks
- Ethical and reputational risks
- Operational and business continuity risks
- Financial and resource risks

**Risk Assessment Process:**
- Initial risk screening for all AI projects
- Detailed risk assessment for higher-risk initiatives
- Regular risk review and updates
- Integration with organizational risk management

### Risk Mitigation Strategies

**Technical Mitigations:**
- Robust testing and validation procedures
- Human oversight and intervention capabilities
- Monitoring and alerting systems
- Fallback and recovery procedures

**Organizational Mitigations:**
- Clear policies and procedures
- Staff training and awareness programs
- Third-party assurance and auditing
- Insurance and legal protections

### Risk Monitoring

**Ongoing Risk Management:**
- Continuous monitoring of AI system performance
- Regular review of risk assessments
- Incident reporting and learning procedures
- Evolution of risk landscape assessment

## AI Quality Assurance

### Quality Framework

**Quality Dimensions:**
- Accuracy and reliability of AI outputs
- Fairness and non-discrimination
- Transparency and explainability
- Security and robustness
- User experience and satisfaction

### Assurance Processes

**Testing and Validation:**
- Pre-deployment testing across multiple scenarios
- User acceptance testing with real users
- Security and penetration testing
- Bias and fairness testing
- Performance benchmarking

**Ongoing Assurance:**
- Continuous monitoring of system performance
- Regular audits and reviews
- User feedback collection and analysis
- Model performance degradation detection

### Quality Standards

**Compliance Requirements:**
- Adherence to relevant technical standards
- Meeting organizational quality criteria
- Compliance with regulatory requirements
- Alignment with industry best practices

**Improvement Processes:**
- Regular quality reviews and assessments
- Continuous improvement initiatives
- Lessons learned capture and application
- Best practice sharing across organization

## Implementation Recommendations

### Getting Started

**Establish Foundation:**
- Set up basic governance structure appropriate to organizational size
- Define clear roles and responsibilities
- Create initial policies and procedures
- Begin building AI inventory

**Build Capability:**
- Train governance board members on AI concepts
- Develop internal expertise in AI governance
- Establish relationships with external experts
- Create templates and tools for consistent approach

### Scaling Governance

**As AI Adoption Grows:**
- Expand governance structures to match organizational needs
- Develop more sophisticated risk management approaches
- Implement advanced quality assurance processes
- Enhance reporting and accountability mechanisms

### Continuous Improvement

**Regular Review and Update:**
- Annual review of governance effectiveness
- Update policies and procedures based on experience
- Incorporate lessons learned from projects
- Stay current with evolving best practices and regulations

### Integration with Existing Governance

**Alignment with Organizational Governance:**
- Connect AI governance with existing IT governance
- Integrate with data governance frameworks
- Align with enterprise risk management
- Connect with procurement and commercial governance

## Practical Recommendations

### Immediate Actions

- Establish AI governance board or designate AI representation on existing board
- Begin creating AI systems inventory
- Define basic AI policies and procedures
- Designate Senior Responsible Owners for existing AI projects

### Short-Term Development

- Develop comprehensive risk management framework
- Implement quality assurance processes
- Create ethics review procedures
- Establish reporting and accountability mechanisms

### Long-Term Strategy

- Build mature governance capability
- Develop specialized expertise
- Create comprehensive policy framework
- Establish strong culture of responsible AI use

### Success Measures

- All AI projects have appropriate oversight
- Risk incidents are identified and managed effectively
- Compliance with legal and ethical requirements
- Positive outcomes from AI implementations
- Strong stakeholder confidence in AI governance
