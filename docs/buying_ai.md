# Buying AI

*This section supports Principle 8: You work with commercial colleagues from the start.*

## Overview

The AI supply market is evolving rapidly. It's important to engage with commercial colleagues to discuss partners, pricing, products and services. The Crown Commercial Service (CCS) can provide guidance on procurement processes and market navigation.

## AI Business Cases

Creating a strong business case is crucial for AI project approval and provides decision makers opportunity to assess return on investment.

### Pre-Business Case Steps

**Stakeholder Engagement**
- Discuss project with stakeholders
- Understand if AI needs to be used
- Identify which solutions offer best productivity improvements

**Investment Thresholds**
- Typically £10 million approaches require five-part business case
- Below this threshold, consider agile business cases guidance (CDDO)
- Mandatory assurance for non-business-as-usual spend:
  - Above £100,000 for digital activities
  - Above £1 million for technology activities

### Business Case Resources

**Treasury Green Book (2022)**
- Use for fully fledged five-part business case
- Required for larger scale projects and investments
- Comprehensive guidance for major investments

**Agile Business Cases**
- Developed by Central Digital and Data Office (CDDO)
- For investments below major threshold
- More flexible approach for smaller projects

**Spend Approvals**
- Follow Cabinet Office guidance on getting spend approvals
- Subject to organization's assurance board requirements

## Routes to Market

Consider available routes and commercial agreements to determine most suitable approach based on requirements.

### Compliance Requirements

Must comply with procurement legislation:
- Public Contract Regulations 2015
- Procurement Act 2023 (effective 2025)
- Commercial colleagues can assist with compliance

### Route Options

**Framework Agreements**
- Successful suppliers awarded at launch
- Closed to new supplier registrations
- Prime suppliers can request new subcontractors
- Often divided into lots by product/service type
- Thorough ongoing compliance checks by CCS
- Various buying options including direct award

**Dynamic Purchasing Systems (DPS)**
- Open for new supplier registrations anytime
- Suppliers filterable by categories
- Basic compliance checks by CCS (buyer completes at call-off)
- Further competition only buying option

**Public Sector Procurement**
- Sometimes called 'Find a tender service procurement'
- Requirement to advertise on platform
- Appropriate for bespoke requirements or contractual terms
- Where no suitable standard offering exists

### Available CCS Agreements

**DPS Examples:**
- Artificial Intelligence (AI)
- Automation Marketplace
- SPARK

**Framework Examples:**
- Big Data and Analytics
- Technology Products & Associated Services 2
- Technology Services 3
- Back Office Software
- Cloud Compute 2

**Memorandums of Understanding (MoUs)**
- Preferential pricing and discounts
- Technology landscape coverage (cloud, software, tech products/services, networks)
- Access MoU savings through any route to market
- Contact: info@crowncommercial.gov.uk

## Specifying Your Requirements

Document requirements to tell suppliers what you need.

### Requirement Development Process

**Engage Subject Matter Experts Early**
- Get SME input as soon as possible
- Consider most appropriate AI solution type:
  - Off-the-shelf product
  - Existing technology with bolt-on AI elements (paid/free)
  - Outsourcing AI builds
  - Co-creating AI with suppliers

### Key Requirement Areas

**Problem Statement**
- Start with clear problem definition
- Define what you're trying to solve

**Data Strategy and Requirements**
- Highlight data strategy and requirements
- Focus on data quality, bias mitigation, limitations
- Consider data format and provide dummy data where possible

**AI Approach Understanding**
- Underline need to understand supplier's AI approach
- Consider strategies to avoid vendor lock-in
- Apply Data Ethics Framework principles

**Technical Integration**
- Mention integration with associated technologies or services
- Consider ongoing support and maintenance requirements

**Commercial Considerations**
- Provide budget guidance considering hidden costs
- Consider intellectual property rights ownership for new software
- Consider acceptable liabilities and risk appetite
- Match against draft terms and conditions

### Additional Guidance

- Read 'Selection, evaluation and award' section in Guidelines for AI Procurement
- Use CCS guide on How to write a specification
- Work with commercial team who will lead procurement process

## Running Procurement in Emerging Market

**Market Characteristics**

**Future Planning**
- Express interest in shaping CCS's longer-term commercial agreement portfolio
- Contact: info@crowncommercial.gov.uk

### Regulation and Policy Evolution

Regulation and policy evolving to keep pace with AI development.

**Current Relevant Provisions:**

**Pro-innovation Approach to AI Regulation**
- Government response to March 2023 white paper consultation
- Early steps toward regulatory regime
- 5 principles to guide responsible AI innovation

**Portfolio of AI Assurance Techniques**
- Developed by Responsible Technology Adoption Unit (RTAU) with techUK
- For designing, developing, deploying, or procuring AI-enabled systems
- Real-world examples of AI assurance techniques
- Support for trustworthy AI development

**Introduction to AI Assurance**
- Developed by Department for Science, Information and Technology (DSIT)
- Help private sector implement AI assurance
- Accessible to range of users
- Core assurance definitions and concepts

**AI Management Essentials**
- DSIT guidance for private sector organizations
- Ethical, robust, responsible AI organizational practice
- Self-assessment tool distilling key principles
- Simple baseline requirements for government suppliers
- Planning to embed in government procurement frameworks

## Aligning Procurement and Ethics

Consider and factor data ethics into commercial approach from outset.

### Ethics Guidance Resources

**Data Ethics Framework**
- Appropriate and responsible data use in government and public sector
- Helps understand ethical considerations
- Addresses ethical considerations within projects
- Encourages responsible innovation

**Data Ethics Requirements Checklist for Suppliers**
- Mitigate bias
- Ensure diversity in development teams
- Transparency and interpretability
- Explainability of results

**Public Sector Contract (PSC)**
- Provisions for intellectual property rights
- Data protection requirements
- Equality, diversity and human rights

### Practical Recommendations

**Early Engagement**
- Engage commercial colleagues from outset
- Understand and use existing guidance
- Understand existing routes to market (frameworks, DPSs, MoUs)

**Clear Planning**
- Specify clear requirements
- Plan procurement carefully
- Seek commercial colleague support for evolving market navigation

**Ethics Integration**
- Ensure procurement aligned to ethical principles
- Factor data ethics from start
- Use available ethics frameworks and checklists

**Regulatory Awareness**
- Stay informed about evolving regulatory and policy landscape
- Leverage assurance techniques and frameworks
- Consider future regulatory embedding in procurement
